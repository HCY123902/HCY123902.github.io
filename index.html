About me

I am currently a year 4 undergraduate [at] National University of Singapore, majoring in Computer Science.

My research focus is on Natural Language Processing (NLP). A more specific track that I am keen on is dialogue agents. I enjoy building conversational agents that can benefit the public in various scenarios. My goal is to extend the usage of open domain chatbots to more general settings. Currently, I am working on Converstation Disentanglement and multi-party Response Generation to enable chit-chat agents to work in multi-party dialogues that involve more than 2 speakers. Solving these tasks is crucial for the eventual application of chatbots in group chat and forums, where different topics may be discussed in a simultaneous and interleaved manner.

What I am doing

  • Conversation Disentanglement

    Conversation Disentanglement is the task of separating the utterances in a multi-party dialogue into detached threads, where each thread corresponds to a single topic being discussed.

  • Response Generation

    Response Generation is the task of generating a text to reply to a given message. While there are many works that explore the diversity and smoothness of generation in a 1-1 setting, research multi-party response generation is still at the starting stage.

    Conversation Disentanglement with Bi-Level Contrastive Learning

    In this paper, we propose to formulate Conversation Disentanglement as a clustering task, in contrast to how it is traditionally solved as a pointing task. We use contrastive learning to pull embeddings of utterances from the same session closer and pull away those from different sessions. K means is then used to cluster the utterances

    • We design a bi-level contrastive learning scheme to learn better utterance level and session level representations for disentanglement.
    • We delve into the conversation nature to harvest evidence which supports our model to disentangle dialogues without any supervision.
    • Experiments show that the proposed Bi-CL model significantly outperforms several state of-the-art models both on the supervised and unsupervised settings across datasets.

    Dec 06, 2023

    Disentangle Multi-party Conversations as Reordering with Curriculum

    In this paper, we propose to use reordering as a generalizable strategy to make conversation disentanglement more effective. Reordering has 2 roles. In training, it is used to generate new dialogues and augment the dataset. In inference, it is used recursively to correct the model's prediction.

    • We propose to use reordering for data augmentation and leverage curriculum learning to guide the training process.
    • We design a recursive reordering process during inference to enhance the model prediction accuracy.
    • Experiments show that our proposed techniques can boost the disentanglement effectiveness across different models and datasets, demonstrating their generalizability.
